Creating Metrics

Funnels

There are additional concepts and terms that are commonly used for designing experiments, especially for web-based studies. In a web experiment, you’ll often think of the user funnel. A funnel is the flow of steps you expect a user of your product to take. Typically, the funnel ends at the place where your main evaluation metric is recorded, and includes a step where your experimental manipulation can be performed. For example, we might think of the following steps for someone to purchase a product in an online store:

  • Visit the site homepage
  • Search for a desired product or click on a product category
  • Click on a product image
  • Add the product to the cart
  • Check out and finalize purchase

Overview

What exactly is A/B testing?

A/B testing is a general methodology used online when you want to test out a new product or a feature. And what you are doing is you are going to take two sets of users and shows the existing product or feature to Control Group, and show the new version to the Experiment Group. And you are going to see how did these users respond differently, in order to determin which version of your feature is better.

Metrics for experiment

Two types of checks

Invariant checking(Sanity Checking): Metrics that shouldn’t change across your experiment and your control. These metrics are used to check if the control and experiment groups are comparable.

  • Are the populations the same?
  • Do you have the same number of users across the two?
  • Is the distribution the same?
  • Do you have comparable numbers of users across countries, or by language?
